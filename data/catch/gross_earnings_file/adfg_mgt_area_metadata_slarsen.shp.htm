Description:
Abstract:
This metadata report applies to both the 2001 Alaska Department of Fish and Game (ADF&G) Groundfish Statistical Area Polygons and the 2001 Alaska Department of Fish and Game (ADF&G) Shellfish Statistical Area Polygons. The groundfish and shellfish fisheries share statistical areas west of 144° W.Long., but east of this longitude (Southeast Alaska and Yakutat) the statistical areas for these two fisheries differ. For the groundfish fishery, the statewide groundfish statistical areas apply. For the shellfish fishery, the Southeast Alaska salmon, shellfish, herring, and miscellaneous finfish statistical areas are used. Because of this difference, the groundfish and shellfish statistical areas have been separated into two maps (pvg_stat_2001 and pvi_stat_2001, respectively).

Statistical areas are polygons that divide the waters of the State of Alaska and the adjacent Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) into small units for the purpose of reporting and analyzing fishery harvest. In this version of the groundfish and shellfish statistical areas, which became effective January 1, 2001, many statistical areas were split to better model federal and state fishery areas. In these cases, the old statistical area numbers were retired and the new split statistical areas were assigned new numbers. New statistical areas will have a GSTARTYEAR (for groundfish) or ISTARTYEAR (for shellfish) of 2000 or 2001. If you need to do an historical analysis on retired statistical areas, you will need to use the previous version of the groundfish and shellfish statistical areas, which is applicable to the years 1986 through 2000. These data sets are available for internal ADF&G use as pvg_stat (groundfish), pvistat6 (shellfish outside of Southeast), and pvistat5 (Southeast shellfish); editing of these files for public access is in progress.

Three types of lines comprise the the groundfish and shellfish statistical area lines: a half-degree by one-degree latitude/longitude grid, lines defined in ADF&G regulations, and other dividing lines that are usually based on prominent landmarks or points of land. All lines are referenced to the NAD83 datum.

Each groundfish and shellfish statistical area polygon is uniquely identified by its 6-digit statistical area number (STAT_AREA). For shellfish east of 144° W.Long, the Southeast Alaska 5-digit statistical area numbers have been padded with a leading zero to make them a 6-digit code.

Purpose:
Participants in the groundfish and shellfish fisheries are required to report the statistical area where harvest occurs. This data set defines the current valid statistical areas that may be used on fish tickets. In combination with summary harvest reports from the ADF&G Neptune (groundfish) and Venus (shellfish) fish ticket systems, this data set is also useful for visualizing and analyzing spatial patterns in harvest. This data set should NOT be used for navigation or for determining compliance with ADF&G Commercial Fishing Regulations. Please consult the ADF&G Groundfish Commercial Fisheries Regulations or the ADF&G Shellfish Commercial Fisheries Regulations for the official definitions of regulatory boundaries.

Data_Quality_Information:
Attribute_Accuracy:
Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
All attributes have been checked for accuracy. Be advised that the ADF&G groundfish and shellfish statistical areas may be split between ADF&G registration areas and other agency reporting areas such as the National Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS) reporting areas and the IFQ Halibut and Sablefish areas. For the NMFS reporting areas and IFQ halibut and sablefish areas there is an additional column in the attribute table that specifies whether the ADF&G statistical area lies wholly within these areas.
Logical_Consistency_Report: There are no overlapping or duplicate polygons.
Completeness_Report:
Hardcopy statistical area charts were checked for completeness by ADF&G area and regional office staff. No missing statistical areas were reported. All waters within the Alaska portion of the US EEZ south of 68° 30' N.lat. are covered by a groundfish or shellfish statistical area.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The original statistical area lines were converted from a shape file to an Arc/Info coverage. Then each line in this coverage was coded based on its type (e.g. coastline, 3-nautical-mile-line, statistical area grid line, land-based point-to-point statistical area line, and fixed regulatory line).
Source_Used_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_lvg_stat_19990415
Source_Produced_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_gnd09ed_20000209
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The goal of this step was to convert the lines in gnd09ed from NAD27 to NAD83. Because some of the lines in this coverage were based on hard lines of latitude or longitude (statistical area grid lines and regulatory lines), the coverage could not be converted to NAD83 as a whole. The statistical area grid lines and regulatory lines had to be separated from the other line types and defined as referenced to the NAD83 datum with no conversion. The remaining lines were converted to NAD83 using Arc/Info's PROJECT command, and all line types were appended into one coverage (gnd14ap). Then this coverage was edited to fix dangles, overshoots, missing or incorrect arcs, and other line topological errors.
Source_Used_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_gnd09ed_20000209
Source_Produced_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_gnd29ed_20000915
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Polygon centroids were added to the line coverage gnd29ed from pvg_stat. Polygon topology was built and the coverage was edited to fix label errors and fill in missing or incorrect statistical area codes.
Source_Used_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_gnd29ed_20000915
Source_Produced_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_gnd39bd_20000926
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Polygons were split to create new statistical areas. Splits were based on requests from area and regional offices. There should be a hardcopy file for each of these requests that describes the changes. In some cases the new lines were digitized by the area offices and sent to ADF&G CF HQ as a MapInfo table or ArcView shape files. In others the changes were based on coordinates in ADF&G and Federal regulations. The old statistical area numbers were retired and new numbers assigned.
Source_Used_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_gnd39bd_20000926
Source_Produced_Citation_Abbreviation: ADF&GCF_gnd60cn_20001122
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Projected coverage to geographic and created shapefile. Merged shapefile with pvsstat1 and pvsdist1 so that shapefile contained all valid groundfish and shellfish statistical areas plus district-wide statistical areas for Southeast Alaska (these statistical areas (e.g. 010100) were used historically to record groundfish and shellfish catch when only the district was known. They are currently not valid statistical areas for either fishery). Added attributes to shapefile and populated using values from pvg_stat and from a spreadsheet created by Gail Smith. Edited attributes. Created final shapefiles pvg_stat_2001 and pvi_stat_2001 so that each shapefile contains all current valid statistical areas for the groundfish and shellfish fisheries respectively.
